How do you get here?

The location is ideal for anyone catching the train or the bus. There are excellent road links and the Peace Memorial Hall is in the centre of village for all those walking.

Penkridge train station is a couple of hundred meters away. It'll take 2 minutes to walk from the station. Probably 7 minutes to stagger back.

If you are on a train from the South (via Wolverhampton) you will need to cross under the track

We'll post some useful train times nearer the date or you can check with www.thetrainline.com

There are bus stops on the A449 and there are regular buses from and to surrounding villages, Cannock, Stafford and Wolverhampton.

National Express coaches also stop on the main road with connections to and from Birmingham, Wolverhampton and Manchester

Walk to the village center - you'll be very exited, but don't run - save your strength for lifting heavy beer.

Cross the A449, looking both ways. The entrance to the event is on Pinfold Lane.

Return home along a route that passes the chippy.

Make sure you have a nominated driver!
Don't drink and drive!

From the North

From the M6 come off at Juntion 13 (signed Stafford (S) A449).

Follow the A449 towards Wolverhampton. This will take you into Penkridge - it's about 3 miles.

From the south, west and east

From the M6 come off at Juntion 12 (signed Telford (M54)) then follow the A5 towards Telford.

At the A5 / A449 roundabout (Spread Eagle) follow the A449 into Penkridge (about 2 miles).

Parking

Ample parking is available nearby, see the map for examples. Recommended parking is on the far side of the train station as this is free.

Please check the time limits where you park; certain places are limited and Traffic Wardens do regular patrols.
